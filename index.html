Common Questions

Car Shipping FAQs

Answers to the questions we hear most often. For more detailed information, visit our complete FAQ page.

Full FAQ Page

Car shipping costs typically range from $350 to $1,800+ depending on distance, vehicle size, transport type, and current carrier availability. Short regional routes (under 500 miles) average $350–$600. Cross-country open transport averages $700–$1,400. Enclosed transport adds roughly 40–60% above open transport rates. Read our detailed pricing guide →

Transit times depend on distance: 1–3 days for routes under 500 miles, 3–5 days for 500–1,500 miles, and 5–9 days for routes over 1,500 miles. After booking, most customers see carrier pickup within 1–5 business days. Expedited service reduces pickup windows significantly.

Yes. All FMCSA-licensed carriers are required to carry cargo insurance. We verify insurance documentation before every dispatch. Most carrier policies cover $100,000+ in cargo. We also offer supplemental coverage options for high-value or classic vehicles. Learn more about coverage →

Open transport uses multi-car carriers with no roof or walls — less expensive, highly reliable, used for 95% of all vehicle shipments. Enclosed transport uses fully covered trailers that protect your vehicle from weather and road debris — recommended for classic, luxury, or high-value vehicles. Compare both options →

Most carriers allow up to 100 lbs of personal items in the trunk, below the window line. Items must not be visible through windows, and valuables are strongly discouraged. Personal items are not covered by carrier insurance — ship them separately for protection.
