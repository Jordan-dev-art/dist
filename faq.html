Car Shipping FAQ — Frequently Asked Questions

General Questions

How much does car shipping cost?

Car shipping costs vary by distance, vehicle type, transport method, and season. Short-haul routes (under 500 miles) average $350–$600. Cross-country routes (2,000+ miles) typically run $900–$1,400 for open transport and $1,400–$2,500 for enclosed. Use our free quote tool for an exact estimate.

How long does car shipping take?

Transit time depends on distance. Regional moves (under 500 miles) take 1–3 days. Mid-distance (500–1,500 miles) takes 3–6 days. Cross-country (1,500–3,000 miles) takes 6–10 days. Allow an additional 1–3 business days for carrier dispatch after booking.

Is car shipping safe?

Yes. The vast majority of vehicles arrive in the same condition they departed. All FMCSA-licensed carriers maintain active cargo insurance. We document vehicle condition at pickup and delivery with a Bill of Lading to protect you in the rare event of damage.

Do you ship to all 50 states?

Yes. OK Car Shipping LLC arranges transport to and from all 50 states, including Alaska (via terminal service) and Hawaii (via ocean freight/Ro-Ro vessel).

Booking & Payment

Is a deposit required to book?

No upfront deposit is required. Payment is collected at delivery (cash, cashier's check, or credit card accepted by the carrier). Some expedited bookings may require a broker deposit — this will always be disclosed upfront.

What is your cancellation policy?

You can cancel your shipment at no charge any time before a carrier is assigned. Once a carrier is dispatched, a cancellation fee may apply. See our full cancellation policy.

How do I get a quote?

Use our online quote form for an instant estimate, or call (405) 555-1234. We typically respond to online inquiries within minutes during business hours (Mon–Sun, 8am–8pm CT).

Transport Types

What is the difference between open and enclosed transport?

Open transport places your vehicle on an open multi-car trailer (the type you see on highways). It's safe, reliable, and the most affordable option. Enclosed transport uses a fully covered trailer, protecting your vehicle from weather and road debris. Enclosed costs 40–60% more and is recommended for luxury, classic, or high-value vehicles.

What is door-to-door car shipping?

Door-to-door service means the carrier picks up your vehicle at your home or office and delivers it directly to the destination address — rather than requiring you to drop off/pick up at a terminal. Most of our shipments are door-to-door. Learn more about door-to-door car shipping.

Can you ship a non-running vehicle?

Yes. Non-running vehicles require special equipment (a winch or forklift). There is typically a $150–$250 surcharge. Please disclose that your vehicle is non-operational when requesting your quote so we can match you with the right carrier.

Insurance & Claims

Is my car insured during transport?

All FMCSA-licensed carriers are required to maintain active cargo insurance covering your vehicle during transport. We verify carrier insurance before dispatch. We also recommend keeping your personal auto insurance active as supplemental coverage.

What should I do if my car is damaged during shipping?

Note any damage on the Bill of Lading before signing at delivery. Do not sign a clean Bill of Lading if damage is present. Contact our customer support team immediately at (405) 555-1234 or dispatch@okcarshipping.com and we will guide you through the claims process with the carrier's insurance.
