Oklahoma to Arizona Auto Transport Overview

The Oklahoma to Arizona car shipping route spans approximately 1,200 miles and crosses through the heart of the United States. OK Car Shipping LLC coordinates this route regularly, with carrier availability that keeps pricing competitive and transit times reliable.

Most vehicles on this route travel via open multi-car carriers, which hold 7–9 vehicles and follow major interstate highways. For luxury, classic, or exotic vehicles, we also offer enclosed transport with full weather and debris protection.

How Long Does Oklahoma to Arizona Car Shipping Take?

The Oklahoma to Arizona route covers 1,200 miles. Under normal conditions, transit takes 3–5 business days once the carrier picks up your vehicle. Factors that can affect timing include:

  • Carrier availability — Pickup scheduling typically takes 1–3 business days after booking
  • Weather conditions — Mountain passes, storms, or winter weather can add 1–2 days
  • Route stops — Multi-car carriers often have multiple stops along the route
  • Expedited options — Guaranteed pickup within 24–48 hours available for an additional fee

Open vs. Enclosed Transport for the Oklahoma to Arizona Route

Open transport is the most popular choice for this route. Your vehicle rides on an open trailer alongside other vehicles. This is safe, reliable, and the most affordable option — accounting for 90%+ of all car shipments on this corridor.

Enclosed transport provides a fully covered trailer, protecting against road debris, weather, and UV damage. It's recommended for vehicles worth over $50,000, classic or collector cars, low-clearance sports cars, or any vehicle with paint work that requires extra care.

Preparing Your Car for the Oklahoma to Arizona Shipment

  1. Remove personal items from the interior (not covered by cargo insurance)
  2. Leave approximately ¼ tank of fuel (enough to load/unload, but not too heavy)
  3. Document existing scratches, dents, or damage with photos before pickup
  4. Disable car alarms or provide the carrier with the deactivation code
  5. Ensure the vehicle is operational — non-running vehicles require special equipment
  6. Retract or remove any aftermarket antennas, spoilers, or accessories that may be at risk

Frequently Asked Questions — Oklahoma to Arizona Car Shipping

How much does it cost to ship a car from Oklahoma to Arizona?

Open transport typically runs $800–$1000. Enclosed transport is $1150–$1450. Pricing varies based on vehicle size, trailer type, and carrier availability at the time of booking.

How long does it take?

Average transit is 3–5 business days for the 1,200-mile route. Add 1–3 days for carrier pickup scheduling after booking.

Can I ship a non-running vehicle on this route?

Yes. Non-running vehicles require a carrier with a winch or forklift. There's typically a $150–$200 surcharge. Let us know at booking so we can match you with the right carrier.

Do you offer door-to-door service on this route?

Yes, door-to-door is standard on most Oklahoma to Arizona shipments. Some residential streets may not be accessible to large carriers — in those cases, we coordinate a nearby meet point.
